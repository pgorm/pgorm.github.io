Four Books I Enjoyed this Spring

  • Never Split the Difference by Chris Voss – Great book highlighting the importance of negotiation in business and life. Chris explains the ideas well and communicates them through high stake scenarios where he was the lead hostage negotiator for the FBI. My favourite concept covered in the book was Black Swans (unknown unknown’s) and how they can affect the strategy. 5/5

  • The Power of Habit by Charles Duhigg – Highlights the importance of identifying habit cycles in life (Cue – Routine – Reward). I though the importance of belief (>2 people) in following through with a habit was powerful when thinking about what habits I have stuck with in my life. Keystone Habits (simple habits which have a big impact on other areas of life) were interesting too! 4/5

  • Bad Blood by John Carreyrou – Fascinating book that reads like a fiction thriller. Important to read for understanding the value of having strong business ethics. The energy of working in a startup, pivoting ideas and personal branding were also exciting to read about. 4.5/5

  • Shoe Dog by Phil Knight – Fantastic read. Inspiring story with great advice for entrepreneurship, business and life. The importance of running in Phil’s life was something I could relate to a lot. Simple acts of gratitude, breaking the rules and grit were key topics too. 5/5
